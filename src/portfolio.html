My own website

Date
Spring 2015
Role
Design and development
Type of Project
Portfolio

His story

Jiayi, known also as Steph, is a strange guy who approached web development in 2013 and got bewitched since then. He loves being updated with trends and knowledge, learning from the best international developers and magazines. Despite his young age, he worked hard to push himself so far.
Always divided between mind and heart, he is always seeking for challenges capable of conveying strong feelings and making his head spin.
In his spare time Jiayi is fond of books of different genres and, although his native language is Italian, he is used to reading English authors in original such as Joyce, Fitzgerald or Wilde.

The challenge

Jiayi is a very precise customer. He pretends everything to be perfect and clear, according to his ideas. He demanded for a website which could show his portfolio and skills as both designer and front-end developer, but that was far from being enough. Conveying his passion for his work had to be the crucial aspect. Moreover he wished to be kept in mind by whoever visited the website.

Outcome

I realized a design based on a modern style, with geometric and rational shapes whereas the colors, backgrounds and images helped to transmit a feeling of magic and joy. Google Web Starter Kit suited as framework, according to Google's guidelines whereas several JS libraries such as GSAP enchanted the visual impact.
Words do have a great power and we chose carefully each single word, in order to communicate his love for web design and development. The outcome is the result of our efforts and it cannot leave no marks on anyone who beholds it.

His story

My Fantasy eBook is a forum born during Christmas Day in 2012, whose aim was to gather addicted book readers of all ages and genres. Differences make people unique, the only requirement is the will to share your love for books, especially fantasy novels. Nowadays My Fantasy eBook is a large community of thousands readers, blended by common passions apart from reading.

The challenge

David Guita, chosen as web graphic, designed a gorgeous fantasy theme, inspired by famous villains like Maleficent, whereas I was charged to develop it and make it load smoothly. The challenge was building this wounderful theme without making the page size too heavy due to endless details and decorations. Moreover I was asked to write a clean and comprehensible code, allowing easy changes in the future even without my support.

Outcome

Work still in progress. Rome wasn't built in a day.

Yiruma

Date
Autumn 2014
Role
Design & Development
Type of Project
Musician Artist

His story

Lee Ru-ma, better known by his stage name Yiruma, is a pianist and composer from South Korea. Yiruma frequently performs at sold-out concerts in Asia, Europe and North America. His alma mater, King's College London, helped him gain European popularity and recognition. Among his most popular pieces are "River Flows in You", "Kiss the Rain", and "Maybe". Yiruma's most popular album, First Love, was released in 2001.

The challenge

This is a personal project, which I realized as a tribute towards my favourite pianist and as an opportunity to improve my technical skills. His songs have always the power to calm down my soul or make it wander above the skies of immagination. I wanted to convey this sensation of delicacy and interior struggle, aside from showing his albums and life.

Outcome

My choice fell upon 'black & gold' as colour palette, since the former gives a wistful atmosphere whereas gold reminds elegancy and sophistry. Music was the essential feature of the website and I wanted the visitor to get in touch with it as soon as possible. To achieve this aim I placed a background video from the very beginning, giving the opportunity to listen to and see Yiruma while he plays his most famous song.
As framework I used Bootstrap, well-known for responsive development, while Spotify APIs allowed users to listen Yiruma's albums.

Sara Lunardi

Date
Winter 2015
Role
Front-end developer
Type of Project
Writer

Her story

Sara was born and raised in the beautiful seaside town of Genoa, Italy, where she studied for a degree in Modern Languages and Journalism, specializing in psychology and sociology. After spending a few years in Switzerland, she moved to London, a city that she has made her home and which provides the setting for much of her writing.
Ecliptic Saga is her true debut, an urban fantasy-meets-science fiction series for young and adult readers.

The challenge

Sara needed a redesign of her previous multi-language website, as she was about to publish her second novel and she felt her presence of the web required a boost and improvement. Her previous website was unable to reflect the beauty of her novels and besides it was still based on a obsolete system, which was heavily limiting in functionality.

Outcome

WordPress was the answer to her needs, since she asked me also as easy way to publish and manage blog articles. By the customization of an up-to-date premium theme, I realized a website which was focused on her books and the magic inside them. The homepage contains the main features of Ecliptic Saga, providing also an easy access to web stores where readers can buy the novels. Secondary pages reveals more deep aspects about the saga and the author.

World War I

Date
Autumn 2014
Role
Front-end developer
Type of Project
Cultural Project

The Project

World War I is a school project, developed in order to remember the Great War since 2014 is the centenary of the outbreak. As the time flows we shall not forget the tragedies of the war and the lives lost in the fight.

The challenge

History telling can be very boring when you are not interested in an endless list of chronological events. The challenge was making the visitor live again the years of the Great War, without nevertheless being boring or flat. As always I hoped to convey some emotions, to transmit a sense of loss.

Outcome

Black & White as colours remind both newspapers during those years and the absence of life during the war. There is no place for bright colours when we are talking about one of the darkest moments of mankind. The events of the war are described in the within a day, to make it lighter whereas the sense of drama is hinted by a symphony by Chopin. Last but not least, an interactive geochart shows the number of soldiers died during the war.
